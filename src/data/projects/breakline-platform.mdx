BreakLine's platform is two-sided. Talent pool members use it to manage their
profiles, track opportunities, and progress through the program. The internal
team uses it to match candidates with partner company roles, run interview
pipelines, and coordinate hiring.

Across nearly three years as the primary frontend engineer, I built the design
system from scratch, shipped the platform's major features, and rewrote the
transactional email system. The stack evolved significantly throughout this
period, including React Router migrations, a transition from Create React App to
Vite, a shift from REST to tRPC, and a restructure from a single React app into
a Turborepo monorepo with five apps and a shared design system.

On a small product team, I owned features from architecture through release and
drove the developer experience infrastructure, major refactors, and tooling
improvements that kept the foundation solid for rapid iteration.
